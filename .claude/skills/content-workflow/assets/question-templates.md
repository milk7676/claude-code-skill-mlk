# Question Templates for Requirements Gathering

This file contains structured question templates for collecting user requirements. Use these templates to systematically gather all necessary information before starting content creation.

## Basic Requirements Template

Use this for standard content creation projects.

### Topic and Content
1. **What is the main topic or subject?**
   - [User provides topic]

2. **What is the purpose of this content?**
   - Inform/Educate
   - Persuade/Convince
   - Inspire/Motivate
   - Entertain/Engage
   - Other: _______

3. **What are the 3-5 key points you MUST cover?**
   - Point 1: _______
   - Point 2: _______
   - Point 3: _______
   - Point 4: _______
   - Point 5: _______

### Audience
4. **Who is the target audience?**
   - Industry/Role: _______
   - Knowledge level: Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced
   - Age range: _______
   - Size: Small group / Large audience

5. **What does the audience already know about this topic?**
   - Nothing - start from basics
   - Some familiarity - brief overview needed
   Very knowledgeable - dive deep quickly

### Format and Length
6. **Article length?**
   - Short: 500-800 words
   - Medium: 1000-1500 words
   - Long: 2000+ words

7. **Presentation length?**
   - Quick: 5-10 minutes (5-10 slides)
   - Standard: 15-20 minutes (15-20 slides)
   - Extended: 30+ minutes (25+ slides)

### Style
8. **What tone should the content have?**
   - Formal/Academic
   - Professional/Business
   - Conversational/Friendly
   - Inspirational/Motivational
   - Technical/Expert

9. **Any specific style preferences?**
   - Minimal text, more visuals
   - Data-heavy with charts
   - Story-driven narrative
   - Practical how-to focus
   - Other: _______

### Materials
10. **Do you have reference materials to upload?**
    - PDF documents
    - Word documents
    - Research papers
    - Notes or outlines
    - Links to resources

11. **What should I prioritize from the references?**
    - Key statistics/data
    - Specific quotes
    - Case studies
    - Methodologies
    - All of the above

### Deliverables
12. **What do you need delivered?**
    - Article only
    - Presentation only
    - Article + Presentation
    - Article + Presentation + Speaker Script
    - Full package (everything above)

## Specialized Variations

### Academic/Research Presentation
Additional questions:
- What is the research question or hypothesis?
- What methodology was used?
- What are the key findings?
- What are the limitations?
- What are the implications or recommendations?
- Citation style required? (APA, MLA, Chicago, etc.)

### Business/Proposal Presentation
Additional questions:
- What problem are you solving?
- What is your solution/approach?
- What is the ROI or business value?
- What is the timeline?
- What is the budget/resource requirement?
- What decision do you want the audience to make?

### Training/Educational Content
Additional questions:
- What are the learning objectives?
- What should learners be able to DO after this?
- What prior knowledge is assumed?
- Will there be exercises or activities?
- How will you measure understanding?
- Any prerequisites or follow-up training?

### Product Launch/Promotion
Additional questions:
- What is the product name and category?
- What are the key features (top 3-5)?
- What are the primary benefits/value props?
- Who is the target customer?
- What is the pricing or call-to-action?
- What differentiates from competitors?

## Quick Start Template

For rapid projects, ask these 5 essential questions:

1. **Topic**: What is this about?
2. **Audience**: Who are they?
3. **Goal**: What should they think/feel/do after?
4. **Length**: How long (article word count / presentation minutes)?
5. **Materials**: Any reference documents to incorporate?

---

**Usage Notes**:
- Start with the template that best matches the project type
- Adapt questions based on user responses
- Not all questions need to be asked - use judgment
- Follow up on vague answers with specific clarifying questions
- Document all requirements before proceeding to content creation
